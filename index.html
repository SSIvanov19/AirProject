Frequently asked questions (FAQ)

What's the difference between PM10 and PM2.5

  • The “10” and the “2.5” refer to micrometers.
  • PM10 is particles 10 microns and below.
  • PM2.5 is particles 2.5 microns and below. (That means PM10 includes PM2.5.)
  • Particulate pollution does not include gas pollutants like ozone and NO2.

What are the norms for PM10 and PM2.5

PM10:
  • ADR [1] - 50 µg/m³ (not to be exceeded more than 35 times a year)
  • AYR [2] - 40 µg/m³
PM2.5:
  • AYR - 25 µg/m³

1. ADR - Average daily rate

2. AYR - Average yearly rate

The impact of polluted air on human health

The dust enters the body mainly through the respiratory system, whereby the larger particles are retained in the upper respiratory tract and the finer particles reach the lower parts of the respiratory system, leading to tissue damage in the lung.

How much electricity the station consumes

  • 1W x 24 hours x 365 days / 1000 = 8.76 kWh or approximately 8.76kW x 0.22 BGN / kWh = 1.92 BGN a year.

How much WiFi internet traffic goes to the station

  • The ESP8266 controller sends very little data in text format (sensor ID, time and hour, and 4 sensor data types), up to a few megabytes a year.

Why the station needs to be constantly connected to WiFi

  • WiFi should be turned on with a real internet connection at all times because the NodeMCU ESP8266, CPU / WLAN has no RAM. If you turn off the WiFi connection, your station data will not be transmitted, displayed and saved.

How to mount a sensor

Make sure the station:
  • Is fixed and stable.
  • Not installed indoors.
  • Not exposed to direct sunlight.
  • It is not on the inside of the balcony and there is sufficient free space below it.
  • Do not smoke near, do not prepare short cuts.
  • No incidental household contaminants are present.

What sensors and controllers are used

The components are:
  • NodeMCU ESP8266 controller, CPU / WLAN.
  • FDP [1] sensor SDS011 NOVA PM.
  • Sensor for atmospheric pressure, temperature and humidity BME280.

1. FP - Fine dust particle
